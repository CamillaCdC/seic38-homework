Norbert Wiener was a great thinker and a tortured person

-Benoit Mandelbrot

Wiener himself, perhaps not unsurprisingly given his upbringing, believed that “society’s understanding of mental illness could benefit greatly from the lessons afforded by the new brain-like computing machines”

He suggested that many human mental disorders were "fundamentally diseases of memory, of the circulating information kept by the brain in the active state"

He explained how modern problems such as "malignant worry", anxiety attacks and other classic neurotic disorders might start with a relatively trivial concern and "build itself up into a process totally destructive to the ordinary mental life"

just as a computer caught up in a logical paradox "may go into a circular process which there seems to be no way to stop"

- Excerpt, Dark Hero of the Information Age by Conway & Siegelman (2005)
